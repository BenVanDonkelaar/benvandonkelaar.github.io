Welcome to Annoyance Analysis!

This is a series of papers written by me exploring the mathematics and physics behind every-day occurrences. It began with when my friend was making fun of me for being bad at the game 'Sudoku', and rather than just getting better, I wrote a paper on the mathematics behind solving a Sudoku game. That sparked the insperation to research into the unseen mathematics behind common games, events and phenomena.

Each paper is inspired by an event that piqued my interest or annoyed me in some way. Feel free to look over the papers, or reach out via the 'contact' page if you have an idea for this series that you would like to see.

DISCLAIMER: These papers are meant to offer mathematical insight in a way that is fun to consume, I attempted to write these papers in a manor that is both academic & formal, but also fun and humerous(for all my serious, formal academic papers, see 'Research Papers' and 'Essays'). I like to call this style of writing (which I have strived to perfect over the years) 'academic shenaniganery', or 'intellectual clownery'. Okay you get the point, happy reading!

DISCLAIMER 2: For the majority of these papers, the topics of which they explore were unknown to me upon the time of writing. These papers are also meant as a learning experiance for me, so please do not expect harvard level papers, they are simply my own research pretaining to an intresting topic or question.
